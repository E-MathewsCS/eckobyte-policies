1. Information We Collect

1.1 Data You Provide

Haventory is a local-first application. Your saved inventory database includes item names, descriptions, values, photos, serial numbers, warranty dates, beneficiary information, and other inventory fields. It is stored on your device in a local database and app-private file storage. We do not operate a cloud inventory account that continuously syncs or stores your complete personal inventory. However, optional online features described below may transmit limited data to third-party services or Haventory backend services when you choose to use those features.

1.2 Data Collected Automatically

  • Service Identifiers: Firebase creates an app user identifier and RevenueCat uses a corresponding customer identifier to manage purchases, entitlements, AI allowances, and fraud controls. These identifiers are not a public profile and are not used by Haventory for advertising. They may be associated with transaction or support records when necessary to operate the service.
  • Purchase Records: When you make an in-app purchase, Google Play and RevenueCat process and store transaction records in accordance with their respective privacy policies.
  • Firebase Authentication and App Check: Haventory uses Firebase anonymous authentication and Play Integrity-backed App Check tokens to authenticate AI requests, protect backend services, enforce usage limits, and reduce fraud or abuse. These services create technical identifiers associated with the app installation and may process device or app-integrity signals.
  • Firebase Analytics and Crash Reporting: We use Firebase Analytics and Crashlytics to collect technical usage, device, performance, and crash information such as app version, device model, operating-system version, screens or features used, and diagnostic events. We use this information to operate, secure, and improve the App; it is not used by Haventory to build an advertising profile.
  • AI Smart Scan: If you choose AI Smart Scan, the selected room images, OCR-derived brand or model hints, and any scan hints you enter are transmitted over encrypted connections to Haventory's secure backend services and then to Google's AI services for processing. Images are processed for the requested scan and are not stored by Haventory as part of your cloud inventory. We do not use scan images for advertising or resale, or arrange human review unless you separately provide content for support.
  • AI Usage and Entitlement Records: To enforce scan limits, subscriptions, credit balances, fraud controls, and cost caps, Haventory backend services store Firebase user IDs, entitlement tier, credit and quota counters, request timestamps, estimated AI cost, and limited RevenueCat event metadata. To prevent duplicate provider charges, AI result records and non-reversible image hashes may be cached for up to 30 days; the cached record can include the AI-generated item drafts but not the submitted image itself.
  • Barcode Lookup: If you use barcode lookup, barcode values may be sent to a third-party product lookup service to retrieve product details.

1.3 Data We Do NOT Collect

  • A name, email address, or phone number as a required Haventory account credential
  • Location or GPS data
  • Contacts, call logs, or messages
  • Browsing history
  • Financial account numbers, credit card numbers, or bank details
  • Health or biometric data

5. Data Storage & Security

  • Inventory data is stored locally on your device using the app's private SQLite database.
  • Photos and attachments are stored in your device's app-private storage directory.
  • Restore-point backup files are created in Haventory's private app storage and are not uploaded or shared by Haventory.
  • Automatic Android cloud backup and device-transfer restore are disabled so an inventory cannot be restored without its attached proof files.
  • We do not transmit your complete manually entered inventory database to our own servers. Optional features such as AI Smart Scan, barcode lookup, purchases, analytics, crash reporting, and backend quota enforcement may involve the third-party services and Haventory backend records described above.
  • Backend records are designed to support entitlement, quota, fraud-prevention, cost-control, and diagnostic functions. They are not intended to serve as a backup of your inventory.
  • AI backend requests use encrypted HTTPS connections, Firebase Authentication, and Play Integrity-backed App Check verification. Server-side authorization, usage limits, and anti-abuse controls are used to reduce unauthorized access and abuse.
  • While we implement reasonable security measures, no method of electronic storage is 100% secure. You are responsible for maintaining the security of your device and backup files.

6. Data Retention & Deletion

  • RevenueCat purchase records are retained as required for subscription management and may persist after uninstallation.
  • AI response cache records expire after up to 30 days under the current service configuration. Quota, entitlement, transaction, webhook, fraud-prevention, security, and diagnostic records may be retained longer as reasonably needed to operate the service, enforce limits, resolve billing or support issues, prevent abuse, and satisfy legal or accounting obligations.
